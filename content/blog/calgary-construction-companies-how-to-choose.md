# How to Choose the Best Construction Company in Calgary: Complete Guide

## What to Look for in a Calgary Construction Company

### 1. **Local Experience**
**Why It Matters:**
- Understands Calgary building codes
- Knows local suppliers and pricing
- Familiar with Calgary climate considerations
- Has relationships with inspectors
- Understands Calgary homeowner preferences

**What to Ask:**
- How long have you been working in Calgary?
- Can you provide local references?
- Are you familiar with Calgary building codes?
- Do you have experience with Calgary permits?

### 2. **Licensing & Insurance**
**Essential Requirements:**
- **Business License**: Must be licensed in Alberta
- **WCB Coverage**: Workers' compensation coverage
- **Liability Insurance**: Protects you and them
- **Bonding**: Additional protection

**What to Verify:**
- Ask for license numbers
- Request insurance certificates
- Verify coverage is current
- Check with licensing bodies

### 3. **Portfolio & References**
**What to Review:**
- Completed project photos
- Similar projects to yours
- Quality of workmanship
- Design capabilities

**What to Ask:**
- Can I see examples of similar work?
- Can you provide references?
- Can I visit a completed project?
- How long have past clients been satisfied?

### 4. **Communication**
**Important Qualities:**
- Responsive to calls/emails
- Clear communication
- Regular updates
- Addresses concerns promptly

**Red Flags:**
- Hard to reach
- Vague answers
- Doesn't return calls
- Poor communication

### 5. **Warranty & Guarantees**
**What to Expect:**
- Workmanship warranty
- Material warranties
- Clear warranty terms
- Stand behind their work

**What to Ask:**
- What warranty do you provide?
- How long is the warranty?
- What does it cover?
- How do you handle warranty issues?

### 6. **Transparent Pricing**
**Good Contractors:**
- Provide detailed estimates
- Explain costs clearly
- No hidden fees
- Fair pricing

**Red Flags:**
- Unusually low quotes
- Vague pricing
- Pressure to decide immediately
- Hidden costs

## Questions to Ask Calgary Contractors

### About Experience
1. How long have you been in business?
2. How many projects have you completed in Calgary?
3. Do you have experience with projects like mine?
4. What's your specialty?

### About Process
1. What's your typical project timeline?
2. How do you handle permits?
3. Who will be on-site daily?
4. How do you communicate during projects?

### About Pricing
1. What's included in your quote?
2. Are there any potential additional costs?
3. How do you handle change orders?
4. What's your payment schedule?

### About Quality
1. What materials do you use?
2. Do you use subcontractors?
3. How do you ensure quality?
4. What warranty do you provide?

## Red Flags to Avoid

### 1. **No License or Insurance**
Never work with unlicensed or uninsured contractors.

### 2. **Unusually Low Quotes**
If it seems too good to be true, it probably is. Quality work costs money.

### 3. **Pressure to Decide Immediately**
Good contractors don't pressure you. Take your time.

### 4. **No References or Portfolio**
Reputable contractors have examples of their work.

### 5. **Asks for Full Payment Upfront**
Normal payment schedules are progress-based, not all upfront.

### 6. **No Written Contract**
Always get everything in writing.

### 7. **Suggests Skipping Permits**
This is illegal and risky.

### 8. **No Local Presence**
Be cautious of contractors with no local office or presence.

## Understanding Quotes and Contracts

### What Should Be in a Quote:
- Detailed breakdown of work
- Materials specified
- Timeline included
- Payment schedule
- Warranty information
- Permit responsibilities

### What Should Be in a Contract:
- Detailed scope of work
- Materials and specifications
- Timeline and milestones
- Payment schedule
- Change order process
- Warranty terms
- Dispute resolution

## Payment Schedules

### Typical Payment Structure:
- **Deposit**: 10-20% to start (materials)
- **Progress Payments**: Based on milestones
- **Final Payment**: Upon completion and satisfaction

### Red Flags:
- Asking for full payment upfront
- Cash-only payments
- No payment schedule
- Pressure to pay before work complete

## Working with Subcontractors

### Questions to Ask:
- Do you use subcontractors?
- Are they licensed and insured?
- Who manages them?
- Are they part of your regular team?

### What to Know:
- Many contractors use subs for specialized work
- Good contractors manage subs well
- You should know who's working on your project
